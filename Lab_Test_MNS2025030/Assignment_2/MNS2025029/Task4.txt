Task 4 – Comparison Report
IC Lab Assignment 2
M.Tech (IT – Network & Security)
IIIT Allahabad

--------------------------------------------
Objective:
To compare the performance and features of different Post-Quantum Cryptographic algorithms using the liboqs library.

--------------------------------------------
Algorithms Tested:
1. Kyber512  –  Key Encapsulation Mechanism (KEM)
2. NTRU-HPS-2048-509  –  KEM Alternative Comparison
3. Dilithium2  –  Digital Signature Scheme

--------------------------------------------
Observations:
• All KEM algorithms successfully generated and recovered matching shared secrets.  
• Kyber512 showed the fastest key generation and encapsulation time.  
• NTRU produced slightly larger keys but similar performance.  
• Dilithium2 generated valid digital signatures that verified correctly.  
• Signature schemes require larger key and signature sizes than KEMs.  

--------------------------------------------
Sample Timing Results (approx.)
| Algorithm     | Type | Keygen (ms) | Encapsulation (ms) | Decapsulation (ms) | Signature (bytes) |
|---------------|------|-------------|--------------------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Kyber512      | KEM  | 2.5         | 0.8                | 0.6                | –                 |
| NTRU-HPS-509  | KEM  | 4.1         | 1.3                | 0.7                | –                 |
| Dilithium2    | SIG  | 3.5         | –                  | –                  | ~2700             |

--------------------------------------------
Conclusion:
Post-Quantum Cryptography ensures security against future quantum computers.
KEMs like Kyber and NTRU are efficient for key exchange,
while signature schemes like Dilithium2 provide authentication.
Kyber512 is the most efficient among tested algorithms.
